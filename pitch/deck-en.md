## Slide 1 · Hook + Problem

**Title: I made none of my own videos — because 80% of it isn't creating**

Key points:
- Every video I ship: I didn't edit it, didn't make the thumbnail, didn't write the caption. Agents did. I'm non-technical
- The real pipeline of one video: pick topic → write → shoot → edit → thumbnail → captions for three platforms → upload to each
- The only real creating is the topic and the shoot. The rest is repetitive grunt work — hours per video, solo. Post daily and you've opened an unpaid sweatshop with yourself inside

Talk track:
> A counterintuitive start. The videos I post — I didn't edit them, didn't make the thumbnails, didn't write the captions. Agents did all of it, and I'm non-technical. Here's why that matters: one video means writing, editing, cutting a thumbnail, a separate caption for Rednote, TikTok and X, then uploading to each. The only part that's really creating is the topic and the shoot. The other 80% is grunt work — hours per video. Post daily and you've built yourself an unpaid sweatshop.

## Slide 2 · Solution

**Title: One idea in, a three-platform launch kit out**

Key points:
- Creator OS: the operating system for a creator's one-person company
- 7 modular Agents, like stations on a line: Idea → Script → Shoot → Edit → Cover → Copy → Publish
- Every station works standalone, or chains into a full pipeline
- Each Agent's prompt is external and tunable — anyone can use the template, and once you tune it, it's yours

Talk track:
> Creator OS is the operating system for a creator's one-person company. Seven Agents, like seven stations. You speak an idea; the Script Agent turns it into a hook-driven script; you shoot and log it; Edit makes the cut, Cover makes the image, Copy spits out captions for Rednote, TikTok and X, and it lands on Publish. Each station works alone, or you chain the whole thing. And every prompt is external — you tune it, and it learns your voice.

## Slide 4 · Differentiator + Real data

**Title: An AI that learns your account — and I use it every day**

Key points:
- Others build tools that do the work, but never know if a video will land. Our moat is a calibration loop:
  1. Before publish, the AI blind-predicts performance into an immutable prediction log
  2. Three days after, it auto-pulls real numbers and reconciles against the prediction
  3. The gap is written back into the scoring rubric — sharper on this account every time
- Each creator's calibration data is a private asset nobody can take (CLI version already runs)
- This isn't a slide. I'm the first user. Measured: pre-processing 56 seconds unattended; hands-on ~10 minutes per video (was hours). Thumbnail factory CoverLab is live — 17 templates, cover-factory-web.vercel.app

Talk track:
> Here's where we split from every AI tool. They do the work but never know if a video will land. We have a calibration loop: before you publish, the AI blind-guesses performance into a log it can't edit; three days later it pulls the real numbers and reconciles; wherever it was wrong, that goes back into the rubric. It gets sharper on your account every time, and that data is yours — switch tools and you can't take it. And this isn't hypothetical. I'm the first user. Pre-processing: 56 seconds, hands-off. My hands-on time: about 10 minutes a video, versus hours. The thumbnail factory is already live, 17 templates. Hours to ten minutes — I clocked it myself.

## Slide 5 · Business model + Roadmap

**Title: Agency-in-a-box — from talking-head to every format**

Key points:
- Three phases: (1) now — self-use validation, prove the pipeline and data; (2) near term — subscription "agency in a box" for talking-head and knowledge creators; (3) long term — calibration data becomes the switching moat
- Beachhead: knowledge-focused talking-head creators — most pain, most willing to pay
- Roadmap expands to tutorials, vlogs, podcast clipping — a new format is just a new set of prompt templates
- Tech base: Claude-powered, reusing two open-source projects (OpenMontage for footage retrieval / social-auto-upload for semi-auto publishing). No reinventing wheels

Talk track:
> Business, three phases. Now: self-use validation. Near term: a subscription, sold to talking-head and knowledge creators — a boxed MCN each. Long term: the calibration data is the moat, the more you use it the harder to leave. First cut is knowledge talking-head creators — most pain, most willing to pay. From there we expand to tutorials, vlogs, podcast clipping, because a new format is just a new set of prompt templates. It's Claude-powered, and we reuse two open-source projects, one for footage retrieval and one for semi-auto publishing. No reinventing wheels.
