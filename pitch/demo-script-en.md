# Creator OS · 90-Second Live Demo Script (English)

> Goal: in 90 seconds, show "ships in 10 seconds → full pipeline → human-in-the-loop → human presses publish"
> Principle: less talking, more clicking. Act first, explain second. All Claude-powered.
> Order: caption kit standalone (ships in 10 sec) → pipeline view (walk the flow) → B-roll confirmation screen → launch desk (auto-fill + human presses publish)
> Note: UI button names below are shown as 中文 (English gloss) — the interface is being localized to English and will be unified later.

---

## Pre-demo setup (30 sec before, while Slide 4 is up)

- [ ] Three browser tabs open: (1) caption kit (2) pipeline view (3) launch desk
- [ ] Confirm network connectivity; run the LLM endpoint once to warm it up and avoid cold-start lag
- [ ] Have a ready-made transcript text on hand, paste-ready
- [ ] Plan B assets in place (see end)

---

## Segment 1 · Caption kit ships (0–20 sec)

**Actions:**
1. Switch to the "文案包 (caption kit)" tab
2. Paste a ready-made video transcript into the input box
3. Click "生成 (Generate)"

**What to say (talking while clicking):**
> Let's start with the smallest piece — it works standalone. Here's a transcript from a video. I paste it in, click generate —

(Wait for output, roughly 5–10 sec to ship)

> There. Done. Rednote title, body and tags, TikTok title, an X one-liner, and thumbnail title candidates — all at once. I used to hand-write these platform by platform. Now, ten seconds.

**The key:** let the judges see with their own eyes — one input, captions for three platforms at once. This is the fastest-hitting hook.

---

## Segment 2 · The full pipeline (20–45 sec)

**Actions:**
1. Switch to the "流水线视图 (pipeline view)" tab
2. Sweep the mouse left to right across the 7 stations

**What to say:**
> That caption kit is just one station on this line. The full line looks like this: idea comes in, the Script Agent turns it into a hook-driven script, you shoot and log it, the Edit Agent produces the cut, the Thumbnail Agent makes the image, the Caption Agent produces the three-platform captions, and it all lands on the launch desk.
> Every station works alone, or you run the whole thing end to end. And every Agent's prompt is tunable — tune it, and it learns your style.

**The key:** get the judges to grasp "modular + chainable + tunable" — don't leave them thinking it's a single-point tool.

---

## Segment 3 · B-roll confirmation screen (45–65 sec)

**Actions:**
1. Open the "知识类·自动配 B-roll (knowledge · auto B-roll)" flow inside the Edit Agent
2. Show the candidate footage the AI has already retrieved for each line of the script
3. Scroll through a few, uncheck one that doesn't fit, swap in another

**What to say:**
> Knowledge videos need visuals. The Edit Agent auto-retrieves B-roll for every line of the script and lays it out like this. But I don't take it all — I scan it, this one's off, swap it, this one stays.
> That's our principle: the Agent does the work, the human makes the call. It does 90% of the retrieval grunt work; I do the last 10% of judgment.

**The key:** this segment is the most direct proof of "human judgment guides AI execution" — dead on theme.

---

## Segment 4 · Launch desk · auto-fill + human presses publish (65–90 sec)

**Actions:**
1. Switch to the "发布台 (launch desk)" tab
2. Click "准备发布 (Prepare to publish)" for one platform; show the upload page auto-filled: video, thumbnail, title, body, tags all in place
3. Move the mouse over the "发布 (Publish)" button, hover, don't click

**What to say:**
> Last step, the launch desk. It auto-fills the upload page — video, thumbnail, title, body, tags, all in place. No more manual typing.
> (Hover the mouse over the publish button)
> But this publish button is always pressed by a human. The AI can get everything ready. The one press that sends it — that has to be a person's decision.
> (Press publish, or hold here as the closing, depending on time)
> That's Creator OS. One person, a crew of Agents, a whole media company.

**The key:** end on the "mouse hovering over publish" beat — it nails the theme line.

## Plan B · Recovery playbook (when network / LLM is slow)

| Risk | Symptom | Recovery |
|---|---|---|
| LLM generation hangs | Caption kit clicked, no output | Immediately switch to a pre-run result screenshot/text: "Network's shaky live — here's the same run from earlier, identical content," and keep going |
| Full network drop | No pages load | Switch to a locally recorded 30-sec demo screencast, narrate over it |
| B-roll retrieval slow | Footage list spinning | Skip live retrieval, show the already-composed finished clip: "Here's the result once it's assembled" |
| Over time | Still not at the launch desk by 60 sec | Cut the swap-footage step in Segment 3, jump to the launch desk — the "human presses publish" beat is non-negotiable |

**Iron rule:** however you recover, Segment 4's "the publish button is always human-pressed" must be demoed. It's the memory hook tied to the hackathon theme — never drop it.

**Do before demo:** run every segment once ahead of time and screenshot/screencast it, saved locally. Even with a full network drop, you can walk the whole flow from screenshots.
