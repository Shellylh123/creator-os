PART 1 — 给 MiniMax 的旁白正文（整段复制，别带这行）

This is Creator OS — your one-person media company. The whole interface is bilingual: English or Chinese, one toggle.

Let's start with the smallest piece, because every tool works standalone. I paste in a video transcript, hit Generate — and there it is. Rednote title, body and tags. Douyin title. An X post. Thumbnail title candidates. All at once. This used to take me an hour, platform by platform. Now? Ten seconds.

And that was just one station. Here's the full production line: Idea, Script, Shoot, Edit, Cover, Copy, Publish. I speak a rough idea, and the Script Agent turns it into a hook-driven script. I shoot it. The Edit Agent cuts it. Cover, Copy, Publish. Every station works alone, or chained end to end. And every Agent's prompt is tunable — tune it, and it learns your style.

Knowledge videos need visuals. So the Edit Agent reads the transcript, marks exactly where B-roll belongs, and searches real stock footage for every point. But I don't take everything it gives me. This one's off — swap it. This one stays. The Agent does ninety percent of the grunt work. I do the last ten percent: the judgment.

Last stop — the Publish Desk. One click, and an automation browser uploads the video and fills in the title, the body, the tags. Everything. But watch this button. The Publish button is always pressed by a human. The AI gets everything ready — but the one press that sends it into the world, that's a person's call.

That's Creator OS. Humans judge. Agents execute. One person, a crew of Agents, a whole media company.
